The provided source addresses the inaccurate evaluation of capacity credit (CC) for generalized energy storage (GES), which encompasses both physical energy storage and virtual energy storage (e.g., demand response). Traditional adequacy-oriented dispatch methods either ignore the profit-maximizing, market-oriented strategic behavior of merchant GES or fail to account for decision-dependent uncertainty (DDU). DDU refers to the stochastic reduction in available discharge capacity caused by accumulated occupant discomfort and capacity market incentives, which fundamentally alters baseline operational bounds during emergency calls. Overlooking these factors leads to overestimated CC values and significant response unavailability.

To resolve this, the authors propose a market-oriented risk-averse coordinated dispatch framework integrated into a capacity credit evaluation pipeline. The method is sequentially executed via Sequential Monte Carlo Simulation (SMCS) over a multi-day horizon. First, a pre-dispatch optimization maximizes energy market price arbitrage profits while enforcing a minimum state-of-charge (SoC) withholding threshold to reserve capacity for future capacity market obligations. Second, during emergency states triggered by system net capacity deficits, a risk-averse re-dispatch phase minimizes loss of load using a distributionally robust chance-constrained optimization. This phase explicitly models DDU by dynamically adjusting SoC bounds based on incentive elasticity and a convex discomfort function that incorporates memory from prior capacity calls. Third, a rule-based recovery policy restores SoC to pre-dispatch levels once deficits clear. To handle data scarcity in DDU characterization, the chance constraints are reformulated using a data-driven approximation that substitutes unknown inverse cumulative distribution functions with robust bounds derived from observed sample sizes and distributional assumptions. Finally, the framework calculates practical expected energy not served (EENS) by simulating how theoretical dispatch strategies interact with practical DDU bounds, and introduces a novel Equivalent Storage Capacity Substitution (ESCS) metric to quantify the deterministic storage capacity equivalent to uncertain GES.

The core mathematical formulations govern the dispatch and uncertainty handling. System state transitions are defined by net capacity $NC_t = \sum_i (P_{i,t}^{\text{CG}} + P_{i,t}^{\text{RG}} - P_{i,t}^{\text{LD}})$ and SoC deviation $\Delta SoC_{i,t} = |SoC_{i,t}^{\text{RD}} - SoC_{i,t}^{\text{PD}}|$. The pre-discharge SoC evolution follows $SoC_{i,t+1}^{\mathrm{PD}} = (\eta_{\mathrm{c},i} P_{\mathrm{c},i,t}^{\mathrm{PD}} - P_{\mathrm{d},i,t}^{\mathrm{PD}} / \eta_{\mathrm{d},i}) \Delta t / E_i + (1 - \varepsilon_i \Delta t) SoC_{i,t}^{\mathrm{PD}}$. DDU risk is constrained via $\mathbb{P}(SoC_{i,t}^{\mathrm{DDU}} \leq SoC_{i,t}^{\mathrm{RD}} \leq \overline{SoC}_{i,t}^{\mathrm{DIU}}) \geq 1 - \varepsilon$, where the accumulated discomfort is modeled as $D_{i,t} = \frac{\rho \sum_{\tau=1}^{k-1} D_{i,t_{\tau}^{(k)}}}{k-1} + (1 - \rho) \times \{ \lambda \sum \frac{P_{\mathrm{d},i,\tau}^{\mathrm{RD}}}{\overline{P}_{\mathrm{d},i,\tau} T} + (1 - \lambda) |SoC_{i,t}^{\mathrm{RD}} - SoC_{i,t}^{\mathrm{B}}| \}$. The tractable reformulation replaces the chance constraint with $a_i(\boldsymbol{x})^\top \boldsymbol{\mu}(\boldsymbol{x}) + \psi_K \boldsymbol{r}(\boldsymbol{x}) + \pi_K \overline{F}_x^{-1}(1-\epsilon) \|\boldsymbol{y}\|_2 \leq b_i(\boldsymbol{x})$. ESCS is derived by equating reliability indices: $RI^{\text{ref}} = RI^{\text{test}} \Rightarrow \text{ESCS} = C^{RP} / C^{GES}$.

Numerical studies on the modified IEEE RTS-79 system using 20 years of Elia TSO data demonstrate the framework’s efficacy. The proposed method (M4) achieves the highest practical adequacy, limiting EENS increases to less than 0.2% compared to theoretical baselines, whereas conventional methods exhibit increases ranging from 1.2% to 20.5%. Overlooking DDU causes practical CC declines of 9.5%–13.9% for physical storage and 20.8%–54.2% for virtual storage. The risk-averse approach improves total merchant profits by approximately 18% over standard market-oriented dispatch by reducing capacity market penalties, despite sacrificing roughly $5.6 million in energy arbitrage revenue. The ESCS metric reveals that VES operates at only 40%–60% equivalence to physical ES in practice. CC values are highly sensitive to configuration, peaking at 91.3% for 10% power/12-hour energy physical storage, and reaching 100% for high load-factor virtual storage.

The authors acknowledge several limitations. The proposed optimization incurs higher computational overhead (averaging 1354.0 seconds per simulation day) due to pre-dispatch and DDU reformulation steps, though offline evaluation mitigates practical deployment barriers. The framework assumes price-taker behavior suitable for decentralized merchant resources; large-scale GES would require bidding-then-clearing market models. Additionally, accurate DDU parameter learning depends on historical response data, which may be scarce, necessitating the use of distributionally robust approximations that trade off conservatism for tractability. Future extensions are required to integrate detailed network constraints, heterogeneous renewable technologies, and co-planning optimization.
