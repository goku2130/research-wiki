# Summary: The False Promise of Imitating Proprietary LLMs

### Core Problem
The authors investigate whether the capabilities gap between proprietary Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, and weaker open-source models can be bridged through "model imitation"—the process of fine-tuning an open-source model on outputs generated by a proprietary system. The central hypothesis is that while imitation can mimic the *style* and *persona* of a strong model, it fails to distill its underlying *factuality* and *reasoning capabilities*.

### Method and Recipe
The researchers trained a series of imitation models using varying base model sizes ($1.5\text{B}$ to $13\text{B}$ parameters), including GPT-2 and LLaMA.

**1. Dataset Construction**
*   **Task-Specific (Local) Imitation:** To create the `NQ-synthetic` dataset, the authors used 10 seed QA pairs from the Natural Questions (NQ) validation set. They prompted ChatGPT to iteratively generate 6,000 additional similar examples.
*   **Broad-Coverage (Global) Imitation:** The `ShareGPT-Mix` dataset was compiled from three sources:
    *   **ShareGPT:** $\sim 50\text{K}$ deduplicated English dialogues.
    *   **HC3:** $\sim 27\text{K}$ ChatGPT responses.
    *   **Discord ChatGPT Bots:** $10\text{K}$ input-output examples.

**2. Training Procedure**
*   **Data Processing:** Conversations were chunked into $2048$ token blocks with special tokens demarcating user queries and model outputs.
*   **Optimization:** Models were fine-tuned for one epoch using the AdamW optimizer.
*   **Hyperparameters:** 
    *   Learning rate: $2 \times 10^{-3}$
    *   Warm-up: $1000$ steps of linear warm-up from $0$
    *   Batch size: $32$
    *   Loss: Standard LM loss applied only to model outputs.

**3. Evaluation Framework**
*   **Automatic Benchmarks:** 5-shot MMLU, 3-shot Natural Questions (NQ), and 0-shot HumanEval.
*   **Human Evaluation:** Blind pairwise comparisons via Amazon Mechanical Turk ($\sim 70$ workers, $255$ held-out prompts).
*   **LLM Evaluation:** Blind pairwise comparisons using GPT-4.

### Key Quantitative Results
The study reveals a stark discrepancy between perceived quality (style) and actual performance (capability):

*   **Stylistic Mimicry:** Crowdworkers rated $\sim 70\%$ of the imitation models' outputs as equal or better than ChatGPT. Table 2 shows that with $150\text{M}$ tokens of imitation data, models achieved an authoritative tone of $98\%$ (matching ChatGPT's $98\%$) and used lists in $81\%$ of cases (compared to ChatGPT's $83\%$).
*   **Capability Gap:** Broad-coverage imitation models showed little to no improvement over base LLaMA models on MMLU, HumanEval, and NQ, regardless of the amount of imitation data (up to $150\text{M}$ tokens). In some cases, performance actually regressed.
*   **Local vs. Broad Success:** Local imitation was significantly more effective. For LLaMA-13B, NQ accuracy increased from $20\%$ (base) to $27\%$ (NQ-synthetic), closing the gap toward ChatGPT's $31\%$.
*   **Scaling Leverage:** Increasing the base model size provided substantial accuracy improvements, whereas increasing the amount of imitation data did not.

### Stated Limitations
The authors identify several potential confounders and limitations:
*   **Contamination:** Evaluation tasks may have been present in ChatGPT's original pre-training data, potentially inflating its scores.
*   **Methodological Scope:** The study focused on supervised learning; it did not explore more complex alignment techniques like RLHF or Constitutional AI.
*   **Data Collection:** The authors used simple collection methods and did not employ advanced strategies such as active learning.
*   **Inherited Weaknesses:** Imitation models inherit the biases, weaknesses, and "black-box" nature of the teacher model.
