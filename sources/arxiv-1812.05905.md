The source addresses two primary limitations of model-free deep reinforcement learning: prohibitive sample complexity and hyperparameter brittleness, which severely restrict real-world applicability. To resolve these, the authors introduce Soft Actor-Critic (SAC), an off-policy actor-critic algorithm grounded in the maximum entropy reinforcement learning framework. Unlike conventional RL that maximizes only expected returns, SAC optimizes a policy to simultaneously maximize reward and entropy, encouraging robust exploration, multi-modal behavior, and stability against model estimation errors.

The algorithmic recipe derives from a maximum entropy variant of policy iteration. First, soft policy evaluation computes the soft state-value function $V(\mathbf{s}_t) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{a}_t \sim \pi} [Q(\mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{a}_t) - \alpha \log \pi(\mathbf{a}_t | \mathbf{s}_t)]$ via the soft Bellman backup operator $\mathcal{T}^\pi Q(\mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{a}_t) \triangleq r(\mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{a}_t) + \gamma \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{s}_{t+1} \sim p} [V(\mathbf{s}_{t+1})]$. Policy improvement minimizes the Kullback-Leibler divergence between the current policy and the Boltzmann distribution of the soft Q-function. For continuous domains, this is approximated using neural networks. The practical SAC recipe employs two independently trained soft Q-functions to mitigate overestimation bias, selecting their minimum for target computation. The stochastic policy $\pi_\phi$ is parameterized via a reparameterization trick $\mathbf{a}_t = f_\phi(\epsilon_t; \mathbf{s}_t)$, enabling low-variance gradient updates. Training alternates between: (1) collecting transitions in a replay buffer, (2) minimizing the soft Bellman residual for both Q-networks, (3) updating the policy by minimizing $J_{\pi}(\phi) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{s}_t \sim \mathcal{D}} \left[ \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{a}_t \sim \pi_\phi} \left[ \alpha \log \left( \pi_\phi (\mathbf{a}_t | \mathbf{s}_t) \right) - Q_\theta (\mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{a}_t) \right] \right]$, (4) automatically adjusting the temperature $\alpha$ via dual gradient descent on $J(\alpha) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{a}_t \sim \pi_t} \left[ - \alpha \log \pi_t (\mathbf{a}_t | \mathbf{s}_t) - \alpha \hat{\mathcal{H}} \right]$ to match a target entropy $\hat{\mathcal{H}}$, and (5) performing soft target network updates with coefficient $\tau$.

Quantitatively, SAC matches or exceeds state-of-the-art on-policy (PPO) and off-policy (DDPG, TD3, SQL) methods across OpenAI Gym benchmarks, particularly on high-dimensional tasks like the 21-dimensional Humanoid where baselines fail to make progress. In real-world applications, SAC learns quadrupedal locomotion for the Minitaur robot in approximately 160,000 environment steps (~400 episodes, ~2 hours of physical training), successfully generalizing to slopes, wooden block obstacles, and stairs without retraining. For dexterous hand manipulation, the algorithm rotates a valve from raw 32×32 RGB images in 300,000 steps (~20 hours), and in 3 hours when provided with direct joint angles, outperforming prior PPO results (7.4 hours) on the same hardware. Standard hyperparameters include two hidden layers with 256 units, Adam optimizer ($3 \times 10^{-4}$), $\gamma=0.99$, a $10^6$-step replay buffer, batch size 256, and $\tau=0.005$.

The authors acknowledge several limitations. Theoretical convergence guarantees for soft policy iteration assume finite action spaces and convex policy classes, whereas practical implementations rely on truncated dual optimization with non-convex neural networks without strict convexity assurances. Real-world deployment introduces non-Markovian dynamics due to system latencies and contact forces, necessitating history stacking of past observations to approximate Markovian states. Additionally, while automatic temperature tuning eliminates per-task entropy hyperparameter sensitivity, other architectural and optimization hyperparameters still require careful configuration. Despite these constraints, SAC demonstrates robust sample efficiency and stability suitable for complex robotic control.
