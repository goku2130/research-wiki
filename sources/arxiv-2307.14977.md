The study investigates whether discrete or "pixelated" spacetime models, which introduce a fundamental length scale such as the Planck length $l_p = 1.616 \times 10^{-35} \text{ m}$, preserve the thermal characteristics of quantum field effects like the Davies-Fulling-Unruh phenomenon. Since spacetime discreteness renders the vacuum a dispersive medium, the authors analyze how modified dispersion relations alter the response of a uniformly accelerated DeWitt-Unruh detector, using it as a proxy for Hawking radiation.

The methodology proceeds through a systematic sequence of quantum field theoretic calculations. First, the authors introduce modified dispersion relations incorporating higher-order momentum terms, specifically $E^2 = p^2 + \kappa \eta^2 p^4 + m^2$ for Doubly Special Relativity (DSR) and an additional $p^6$ term for Hořava-Lifshitz gravity (HLG). Second, they derive the corresponding wave equation and frequency-dependent refractive index $n(k) = \frac{\sqrt{1 + \kappa \eta^2 k^2}}{1 + 2 \kappa \eta^2 k^2}$, demonstrating that $\kappa > 0$ yields superluminal propagation while $\kappa \leq 0$ yields subluminal propagation. Third, the modified Feynman propagator is computed in momentum space and transformed to position space via contour integration, yielding the positive- and negative-frequency Hadamard functions. For time-like separations, the propagator takes the form $D^+(t,r) = \frac{-\theta(\sigma^2)}{4\pi^2(\sigma^2 - i\epsilon)} \left\{1 - \frac{\kappa \eta^2}{(\sigma^2 - i\epsilon)}\right\}$. Fourth, the detector’s hyperbolic trajectory $z = \alpha \cosh(\tau/\alpha), t = \alpha \sinh(\tau/\alpha)$ is substituted into the propagator. Finally, the transition probability per unit proper time is evaluated by integrating the modified propagator along the worldline, utilizing series expansions of $\sinh^{-2}$ and $\sinh^{-4}$ terms and contour integration to sum over frequency modes.

The quantitative results confirm that the detector response retains a strictly Planckian thermal character, with the standard factor $\frac{E - E_0}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1}$ and imaginary time periodicity $\beta = 2\pi\alpha$ preserved. The modified response function including $p^4$ corrections is given by $\mathcal{F}(E) = \left\{\left(1 + \frac{\kappa \eta^2}{6 \alpha^2}\right) \frac{E - E_0}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1} + \left(\frac{\kappa \eta^2}{6}\right) \frac{(E - E_0)^3}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1}\right\}$. When $p^6$ terms are included, the response expands to $\mathcal{F}(E) = \left\{\left(1 + \frac{\kappa \eta^2}{6 \alpha^2} - \frac{3 \kappa' \eta^4}{5 \alpha^4}\right) \frac{E - E_0}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1} + \left(\frac{\eta^2 [\kappa + 12 \kappa' \eta^2]}{6}\right) \frac{(E - E_0)^3}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1} + \left(\frac{\kappa' \eta^4}{10}\right) \frac{(E - E_0)^5}{e^{2\pi (E - E_0)\alpha} - 1}\right\}$, where $\kappa' = \kappa(\frac{3}{4}\kappa + 1)$. Crucially, the authors identify a severe pathology for subluminal propagation ($\kappa < 0$): the transition probability becomes negative when $|\kappa| > \frac{6\alpha^2}{\eta^2}$, signaling a potential breakdown of unitarity at extreme accelerations. Conversely, superluminal cases ($\kappa > 0$) yield a strictly positive response function across all acceleration scales. Higher-order corrections do not cancel these negative terms.

The authors explicitly state several limitations. The framework deliberately violates Lorentz invariance to accommodate a fixed fundamental length, and the analysis isolates the dispersion relation while deferring the full DSR treatment that would require momentum space curvature and modified integration measures. The subluminal pathology may be an artifact of this simplified Lorentz-violating approach and could be resolved in a fully Lorentz-invariant formulation. Additionally, the study assumes $\eta \sim 1/M_p$ and $\kappa = \pm 1$ for qualitative assessment, and it does not yet address how these vacuum dispersion effects quantitatively alter Bogoliubov transformations in black hole radiance calculations.
