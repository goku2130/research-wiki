This paper investigates the duality properties of almost Dunford-Pettis operators between Banach lattices. An operator $T: E \to F$ is almost Dunford-Pettis if it maps disjoint weakly null sequences to norm null sequences. The core problem is to establish precise conditions on the Banach lattices $E$ and $F$, and on the operator $T$, such that the adjoint $T^*: F^* \to E^*$ and the second adjoint $T^{**}: E^{**} \to F^{**}$ inherit the almost Dunford-Pettis property.

The authors execute a stepwise characterization program. First, they characterize when the bidual $E^{**}$ possesses the positive Schur property (where positive disjoint weakly null sequences are norm null) by examining second adjoints of operators mapping into $\ell_\infty$. Theorem 2.1 establishes the equivalence of four conditions: (a) $T^{**}$ is almost Dunford-Pettis for every bounded $T: E \to \ell_\infty$; (b) for every regular $T$; (c) for every positive $T$; and (d) $E^{**}$ has the positive Schur property. The proof constructs a positive operator $T$ via Kantorovich’s extension theorem, utilizing norm estimates such as $\|T^{**}(z_m^{**})\| \geq \alpha \|y_m\| = \alpha > 0$ to demonstrate the property's failure when $E^{**}$ lacks the positive Schur property. Second, the paper addresses whether $T$ almost Dunford-Pettis implies $T^*$ almost Dunford-Pettis. Theorem 3.5 proves that if the adjoint of every positive Dunford-Pettis operator $T: E \to F$ is almost Dunford-Pettis, then $E^*$ has order continuous norm or $F^*$ has the positive Schur property. The construction $T = R \circ S$ with $S: E \to \ell_1$ and $R: \ell_1 \to F$ yields the critical norm bound $\|T^*(y_k^*)\| \geq y_k^*(y_k)x_k^*(x_k) \geq 1/4$. Theorem 3.7 further establishes that if $E^*$ has order continuous norm, every positive almost Dunford-Pettis operator is positively limited, implying $T^*$ is almost Dunford-Pettis. Third, for the second adjoint, Theorem 3.11 provides a unified condition: if $E^*$ and $F$ have order continuous norms, then any positive almost Dunford-Pettis $T: E \to F$ satisfies that $T^{**}$ is almost Dunford-Pettis. The proof relies on Lemma 3.6 and the estimate $0 \leq T^{**}(x_n^{**})(y_n^*) = x_n^{**}(T^*(y_n^*)) \leq \sup_k \|x_k^{**}\| \cdot \|T^*(y_n^*)\| \to 0$. Finally, the authors examine cases where $T^*$ or $T^{**}$ are almost Dunford-Pettis despite $T$ not being. Proposition 4.1 shows every order bounded operator $T: C(K) \to F$ is positively limited, proven via $\|T^*(y_n^*)\| \leq y_n^*(y) \to 0$. Theorem 4.4 proves that if $E$ has the dual positive Schur property, the adjoint of every bounded order weakly compact operator $T: E \to X$ is almost limited, hence $T^{**}$ is almost Dunford-Pettis.

Key quantitative results include the explicit norm thresholds $\alpha > 0$ and $1/4$ used to disprove the almost Dunford-Pettis property in constructed counterexamples, alongside the bound $3M/4$ in the inductive subsequence construction of Lemma 3.8. The analysis is strictly confined to linear operators between Banach lattices, relying on order continuity, Dedekind $\sigma$-completeness, and the canonical embedding $J_E$. The authors explicitly state several limitations: $T^*$ being almost Dunford-Pettis does not imply $T$ is almost Dunford-Pettis, while $T^{**}$ being almost Dunford-Pettis always implies $T$ is. Specific counterexamples demonstrate failures: $\text{id}_{\ell_1}^* = \text{id}_{\ell_\infty}$ fails because $\ell_\infty$ lacks the positive Schur property, and $\text{id}_{E^{**}}$ fails for $E = (\bigoplus_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \ell_n^n)_1$. The paper also notes the non-existence of a Banach lattice $F$ where $F^*$ has the positive Schur property and $F^{**}$ has order continuous norm. The results are positioned as improvements over prior literature, achieving identical or stronger conclusions under strictly weaker assumptions on the underlying lattices.
