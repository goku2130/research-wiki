The paper "Fine-Tuning Language Models with Reward Learning on Policy" by Lang et al. (2024) addresses the challenge of reward model inaccuracy in Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) for Large Language Models (LLMs).

**Core Problem:**
Standard RLHF procedures involve sequential steps: human preference collection, reward learning, and policy optimization. Policy optimization continuously shifts the LLM's data distribution, causing the fixed reward model, trained on offline data, to become inaccurate off-distribution. This degradation in reward model accuracy can lead to the policy exploiting differences between inferred and true rewards, ultimately degenerating the policy. While repeatedly collecting new human preference data from updated policies can mitigate this, it complicates the system and is difficult to optimize.

**Method/Recipe Step by Step:**
The proposed framework, Reward Learning on Policy (RLP), refines a reward model using policy samples to keep it on-distribution without requiring continuous new human preference data collection. RLP consists of five steps:
1.  **Collect a human preference dataset D:** This initial dataset is used for training.
2.  **Train a reward model $r_{\phi}$ using D:** An initial reward model is trained on the collected human preferences.
3.  **Fine-tune a language model policy $\pi_{\theta}$ from an SFT model $\pi^{\mathrm{SFT}}$ using unlabeled data U and $r_{\phi}$:** The initial policy is optimized using the trained reward model.
4.  **Retrain a reward model $\hat{r}_{\phi}$ using policy outputs:** This is the core RLP step, where the reward model is refined using samples generated by the current policy. Two methods are proposed for this:
    *   **Unsupervised Multi-view Learning (UML):** For each instruction $x$ from an unlabeled dataset U, the policy $\pi_{\theta}$ generates two responses $y_1, y_2$. These form two views, $v_1(x) = (x, y_1)$ and $v_2(x) = (x, y_2)$. A multi-view information bottleneck (MIB) loss is optimized to learn robust representations of the policy's data distribution.
    *   **Synthetic Preference Generation (SPG):** For each instruction $x$ from U, the policy $\pi_{\theta}$ generates a set of $n$ outputs $y = \{y_1, ..., y_n\}$. Semantic equivalence is addressed by clustering these outputs into groups $\mathcal{G}$ using a bi-directional entailment algorithm. The largest cluster $\tilde{g}$ is identified, and its size relative to the total outputs, $\frac{|\tilde{g}|}{|y|}$, serves as a confidence score. Synthetic pairwise preferences are selectively generated for instructions where this confidence score exceeds a threshold $\gamma$. The preferred output $y_w$ is sampled from $\tilde{g}$ (with the largest reward score), and the non-preferred output $y_l$ is randomly sampled from the remaining clusters $y \setminus \tilde{g}$.
5.  **Fine-tune a policy $\hat{\pi}_{\theta}$ from $\pi^{\mathrm{SFT}}$ using U and $\hat{r}_{\phi}$:** The policy is retrained based on the refined reward model.

**Key Formulas in LaTeX:**
*   **Reward Learning Loss (Standard RLHF):**

$$
\mathcal{L}_{R}=-\mathbb{E}_{(x,y_{w},y_{l})\sim\mathcal{D}}[\text{l o g}\sigma(r_{\phi}(x,y_{w})-r_{\phi}(x,y_{l}))]
$$

    where $\mathcal{D}$ is the dataset of pairwise preferences and $\sigma$ is the sigmoid function.
*   **Multi-view Information Bottleneck (MIB) Loss for RLP-UML:**

$$
\mathcal{L}_{M}=\mathbb{E}_{(x,\mathbf{y})\sim\mathcal{P}}[-\mathbb{I}(z_{1};z_{2})+\mathbb{D}_{\text{S K L}}(p_{\psi}(z|v_{1})||p_{\psi}(z|v_{2}))]
$$

    where $\mathcal{P}$ is the dataset of policy samples, $\mathbb{I}$ is mutual information, and $\mathbb{D}_{\text{S K L}}$ is the symmetrized KL divergence.
*   **Overall Reward Retraining Loss for RLP:**

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\hat{R}}={{}-\mathbb{E}_{(x,y_{w},y_{l})\sim\mathcal{D}\cup\hat{\mathcal{D}}}[\text{l o g}\sigma(\hat{r}_{\phi}(x,y_{w})-\hat{r}_{\phi}(x,y_{l}))]}+\lambda\:\mathcal{L}_{M}
$$

    where $\hat{\mathcal{D}}$ is the synthetic preference dataset generated by SPG, and $\lambda$ controls the weight of the MIB loss.
    *   For RLP-UML, $\hat{\mathcal{D}}$ is removed, and $\lambda$ is set to 0.5.
    *   For RLP-SPG, $\mathcal{L}_{M}$ is removed (by setting $\lambda=0$).
*   **Policy Retraining Objective:**

$$
\mathbb{E}_{x\sim\mathcal{U},y\sim\hat{\pi}_{\theta}(y|x)}[\hat{r}_{\phi}(x,y)-\beta\mathbb{D}_{\text{K L}}(\hat{\pi}_{\theta}(y|x)||\pi_{\text{r e f}}(y|x))]
$$

    where $\mathcal{U}$ is the unlabeled dataset, $\pi_{\text{r e f}}$ is the reference policy, and $\beta$ is a regularization coefficient.

**Key Quantitative Results and Numbers:**
Experiments were conducted on three benchmark datasets: AlpacaFarm, LLMBar, and Vicuna, using LLaMA-7B as the base model. Win-rates are computed against the reference model `text-davinci-003`.
*   **Overall Performance (Win-Rate %):**
    *   RLP-SPG achieved the highest win-rates among implemented LLMs:
        *   AlpacaFarm (Simulated): 50.2% (compared to PPO 46.8%)
        *   AlpacaFarm (Human): 57.4% (compared to PPO 55.1%)
        *   LLMBar (Simulated): 50.5% (compared to PPO 47.5%)
        *   Vicuna (Simulated): 62.5% (compared to PPO 57.5%)
    *   RLP-UML also outperformed PPO:
        *   AlpacaFarm (Simulated): 49.1%
        *   AlpacaFarm (Human): 56.5%
        *   LLMBar (Simulated): 48.5%
        *   Vicuna (Simulated): 61.3%
*   **Ablation Study on Representation Learning Loss (AlpacaFarm Win-Rate %):**
    *   RLP-UML: 49.1%
    *   InfoMax: 44.4%
    *   MVI: 48.1%
    *   CL: 48.2%
*   **Ablation on Synthetic Preference Generation (AlpacaFarm Win-Rate %):**
    *   RLP-SPG: 50.2% (Preference Acc. 59%)
    *   RLAIF (A Pair): 46.0% (Preference Acc. 52%)
    *   Reward (A Pair): 48.9% (Preference Acc. 56%)
    *   Select-All (A Set): 48.7% (Preference Acc. 55%)
*   **MMLU Benchmark (Score %):**
    *   RLP-UML: 37.6%
    *   RLP-SPG: 37.3%
    *   PPO: 36.9%
    *   LLaMA-7B: 35.2%
    *   ChatGPT: 70.0%
    *   GPT-4: 86.4%
*   **Output Lengths (RLP-SPG):** Preferred outputs ($y_w$) were significantly longer (510 characters on average) than non-preferred outputs ($y_l$) (449 characters on average).
*   **Selective Generation Threshold:** $\gamma = 0.5$ was used for RLP-SPG.
*   **MIB Loss Coefficient:** $\lambda = 0.5$ was used for RLP-UML.
*   **Sampling Temperature:** 1.0 for training, 0.7 for inference.

**Stated Limitations:**
*   The empirical verification of RLP's effectiveness was limited to LLaMA-7B. Its performance when aligning larger pretrained LLMs has not yet been confirmed.
*   The study did not explore aligning with newer state-of-the-art pretrained LLMs like LLaMA 2.
*   Other methods for fitting preference data, such as Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), were not tested.
