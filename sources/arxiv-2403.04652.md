The source introduces the Yi model family, addressing the challenge of developing open, high-performance large language models that match frontier proprietary systems like GPT-3.5 while remaining deployable on consumer-grade hardware with constrained memory (e.g., 24GB VRAM). The core methodology prioritizes data quality and engineering over architectural novelty, operating in the post-Chinchilla optimal regime by training smaller models on excessively large, meticulously filtered datasets.

Pretraining utilizes a standard decoder-only Transformer architecture with Grouped-Query Attention (GQA), SwiGLU activations, and Rotary Position Embeddings with an adjusted base frequency (RoPE ABF). The model is trained on 3.1 trillion tokens of bilingual English and Chinese data. Data curation employs a cascaded pipeline: heuristic filters remove low-quality text, garbled content, and PII; learned filters (perplexity, quality, coherence, and safety scorers) eliminate nuanced harmful or incoherent content; cluster-based semantic filtering identifies low-quality themes; and a deduplication process applies MinHash and exact matching. Tokenization uses Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) with a 64,000 vocabulary. Supervised finetuning strictly emphasizes quality over quantity, utilizing a curated dataset of fewer than 10,000 multi-turn instruction-response pairs formatted in ChatML. Each instance is manually verified and iteratively polished by machine learning engineers. The training objective computes next-word prediction loss exclusively on the response tokens. Optimization employs the AdamW optimizer ($\beta_1=0.9, \beta_2=0.999, \epsilon=10^{-8}$) with a sequence length of 4096, batch size of 64, 300 training steps, a constant learning rate of $1 \times 10^{-5}$, weight decay of 0.1, gradient clipping at 1.0, and NEFTune noise scaling (45 for 34B, 5 for 6B).

Capability extensions include three post-training strategies. Long-context modeling (200K tokens) is achieved via lightweight continual pretraining on 5 billion length-upsampled tokens followed by supervised finetuning on synthetic document QA pairs. Vision-language adaptation (Yi-VL) integrates a CLIP ViT-H/14 encoder with a two-layer MLP projector, trained across three stages: (1) 224² resolution alignment on 100M image-text pairs, (2) 448² resolution refinement on 24.8M pairs, and (3) full-model multimodal chat training on ~1M pairs. Depth upscaling duplicates middle transformer layers (12–28) to expand a 6B model to 9B, followed by continual pretraining on 800 billion tokens. Infrastructure supports full-stack development via cross-cloud elastic scheduling, ZeRO-1 memory partitioning, 3D parallelism, and topology-aware resource allocation. Inference acceleration combines 4-bit model and 8-bit KV cache quantization with PagedAttention and dynamic batching.

Quantitative evaluations demonstrate that Yi-34B achieves GPT-3.5 parity, scoring 76.3 on MMLU (base) and 73.5 (5-shot chat), with an AlpacaEval win rate of 94.08 and an LMSys Chatbot Arena ELO rating of 1110. 4-bit AWQ and 8-bit GPTQ quantization reduce memory footprint to fit on 24GB GPUs with less than 1% performance degradation. Depth upscaling yields Yi-9B, which improves MMLU to 68.4 and GSM8K to 52.3. The in-context learning study evaluates function inference using the linear combination $y = w_1x_1 + w_2x_2 + \dots + w_nx_n$, measuring prediction accuracy via absolute difference $|y - y^*|$ and exact match.

The authors acknowledge several limitations. Open-source models, including Yi, still trail GPT-4 on complex reasoning, coding, and mathematics. Smaller variants (6B) exhibit weaker mathematical capabilities, suggesting insufficient SFT data to activate latent abilities. The data cleaning pipeline, while rigorous, is highly complex and resists exhaustive grid-search optimization, indicating potential for further refinement. Furthermore, the authors note that scaling beyond 3.1T tokens and increasing model parameters with optimized data will likely be necessary to close remaining performance gaps.
